Pirates and treasures

There are five rational pirates (in strict order of seniority 1, 2, 3, 4 and 5, that means 2 depends on 1, 3 depends on 2 and so on) who found 100 gold coins. They must decide how to distribute them.
You propose a plan of distribution. The pirates, then vote on whether to accept this distribution. If the majority accepts the plan or tie vote, the coins are dispersed and the game ends. If the majority rejects the plan, the proposer is thrown overboard from the pirate ship and dies and you lose.
Pirates base their decisions on four factors. First of all, each pirate wants to survive. Second, given survival, each pirate wants to maximize the number of gold coins he receives. Third, each pirate would prefer to throw another overboard, if all other results would otherwise be equal. And finally, the pirates do not trust each other.
